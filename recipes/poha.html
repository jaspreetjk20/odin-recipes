Poha

Description

Poha is a popular Indian breakfast dish made from flattened rice, which is sautéed with onions, mustard seeds, turmeric, green chilies, and curry leaves. It's often garnished with fresh coriander, peanuts, and a squeeze of lemon for added flavor. Light, savory, and nutritious, it is a comforting and quick meal.

Ingredients

  • Flattened rice (poha) - 1 cup
  • Onion - 1 medium-sized, finely chopped
  • Mustard seeds - 1/2 teaspoon
  • Green chilies - 1-2, finely chopped
  • Peanuts - 2 tablespoons

Steps

  1. Rinse the poha: Wash the poha thoroughly in water to remove any dirt. Drain the water and let it sit for about 5 minutes to soften. Gently fluff it with a fork to separate the flakes.
  2. Prepare the tempering: Heat oil in a pan on medium heat. Add mustard seeds, and once they splutter, add curry leaves (if using) and chopped green chilies. Sauté for a few seconds.
  3. Cook onions and peanuts: Add the chopped onions to the pan and sauté until they become soft and translucent. Add the peanuts and cook for another 2 minutes.
  4. Add spices: Sprinkle turmeric powder and salt to taste. Mix well.
  5. Combine poha: Add the rinsed poha to the pan, and gently stir to combine everything evenly. Cook for 2-3 minutes, ensuring that the poha is well-coated with the spices.
  6. Garnish and serve: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice. Serve hot with lemon wedges on the side.
